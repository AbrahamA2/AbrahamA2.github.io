An Archaic Expression of Passion

Art thou a rose?
Who's beauty I may scribe to in prose?
Nay, for shame
For 'tis a rose that is sharp with suffering
I beseech thee, allow me an apology!
Fie! The only thing comprable is thyn beauty
I may, perchance, compare you to the crescent
Many a night in my waking hour I behold the moon
It illuminates the darkness, as do you
''Tis thee, queen of night
Wherefore may I not become a sky sailor?
Forsooth! I would spend mine day by the white smile
Thither she rules, her shine doth outshine stars
During the day, then, I shall slumber
For at nightfall, mine time shall be spent
Laying mine eye on the stone
That gives way for my life

I love you,
Abraham.
